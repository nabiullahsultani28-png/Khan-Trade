Where we came from

Where this came from.

The Khan Trading started with one problem: most traders run a dozen indicators and switch strategy every week, and never get consistent. The fix was to pick one framework — ICT — and turn it into a fixed process: CRT to find the trade, the Unicorn entry for the highest-confluence setups, and a checklist for every entry.

What we do in practice: post live setups during the London and New York sessions with the entry, stop and target, explain the reasoning, and stay on Gold and the FX majors. Trading carries risk, and the decisions you make are your own.

Models

CRT & Unicorn

CRT reads one reference candle's range to define entry, stop and target in four steps. The Unicorn entry stacks a breaker block, a fair-value gap and a liquidity sweep for the highest-confluence setups (typically 1–3 a week).

How we trade

Four rules we hold to.

Session-timed

Setups are taken in the London and New York sessions. Outside them, we stay flat.

Two models only

We trade CRT and the Unicorn entry — not a rotating set of indicators.

Checklist entries

Every entry follows a fixed checklist: sweep, displacement, entry zone, stop, target. No checklist, no trade.

Risk first

Risk per trade is fixed and defined before entry. Capital preservation comes before profit.

Where we trade

Gold and the FX majors. Nothing else.

We trade XAUUSD, EUR/USD and GBP/USD during the London and New York sessions. No crypto, no rotating markets — these are the instruments CRT and the Unicorn entry are tuned for.
