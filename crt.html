CRT Model

Candle Range Theory, traded with rules.

CRT uses one meaningful candle as the map. Its high and low create CRH and CRL, the midpoint gives EQ, and the trade only becomes interesting after price sweeps one side and returns back inside the range.

The actual model

Anchor, sweep, reclaim, deliver.

CRT is not a breakout signal. It is a liquidity model built from a reference candle. The candle gives the range, the sweep exposes the trapped side, and the return inside the range decides whether a controlled trade plan exists.

  1. 01

    Anchor one meaningful candle

    Use a candle with context: previous day, previous session, weekly open range, or a higher-timeframe candle. The high becomes CRH, the low becomes CRL, and the midpoint becomes EQ.

  2. 02

    Wait for manipulation outside the range

    Price should raid liquidity beyond CRH or CRL first. A sweep above CRH creates a possible short only after rejection; a sweep below CRL creates a possible long only after rejection.

  3. 03

    Require the range to be reclaimed

    The swept move must close or trade decisively back inside the candle range. That re-entry is the difference between a trapped breakout and a clean continuation through the level.

  4. 04

    Plan delivery before entry

    Risk belongs beyond the swept extreme. The first logical objective is EQ; if price accepts through the midpoint, the opposite side of the range is the next draw - a CRH sweep targets CRL, a CRL sweep targets CRH.

3-candle sequence · Power of 3

Accumulation, manipulation, distribution.

CRT is the ICT Power of 3 (AMD) read on a single candle. Candle one accumulates and defines the range, candle two manipulates - running liquidity outside the range - and candle three distributes, delivering price away from the raid toward the opposite side.

01

Range candle

Accumulation. Build the map - mark CRH, CRL and EQ before the move develops.

02

Manipulation candle

Manipulation. Price runs outside the range to raid buy-side or sell-side liquidity, then rejects.

03

Delivery candle

Distribution. Re-entry and displacement deliver price back through the range toward the opposite draw.

Range anatomy

One candle gives the whole map.

The reference candle splits the chart into premium, discount and equilibrium. The model only matters when price raids one side, returns inside, then delivers away from the trapped liquidity.

  • CRH Candle Range High
  • EQ Equilibrium / midpoint
  • CRL Candle Range Low
  • Raid Liquidity taken outside the range
Valid vs invalid

The filter should reject weak stories.

A clean CRT setup is obvious before entry. If the range was selected after the move, if price never reclaims the range, or if the stop only works after being moved, the setup is not valid.

Valid setup

  • Reference candle selected before the sweep
  • Liquidity raid beyond CRH or CRL
  • Re-entry back inside the candle range
  • Displacement away from the swept side
  • Risk placed beyond the swept extreme

Invalid setup

  • Range chosen after price already delivered
  • Clean breakout being forced into a reversal idea
  • No return back inside CRH or CRL
  • No displacement after the reclaim
  • Stop or target moved to make the trade fit
Practice loop

Build the eye before you risk money.

Replay old sessions candle by candle. Mark the reference range before the sweep, document whether price re-entered, then score the delivery to EQ and the opposite side in R. The sample tells you which sessions and pairs deserve attention.

  1. 01 Mark 30 reference candles
  2. 02 Screenshot every CRH or CRL sweep
  3. 03 Log re-entry, displacement and target delivery
