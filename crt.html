Candle Range Theory

A range model for cleaner decisions.

CRT starts with one reference candle. Its high and low become the map, the sweep shows where liquidity was taken, and the re-entry decides whether the trade is worth planning.

CRT in 4 rules

The model is simple on purpose.

The point is not to decorate a chart. The point is to create a repeatable decision: identify the range, wait for liquidity to be taken, require proof back inside the range, then manage risk before entry.

  1. 01

    Choose a clean candle

    Use a reference candle that actually matters: previous day, active session, or a higher-timeframe candle. Its high is CRH. Its low is CRL.

  2. 02

    Let price take liquidity

    Price should pierce one side of the range first. A breakout that keeps running is information, not an automatic entry.

  3. 03

    Require re-entry

    The swept move needs to trade back inside the range and show intent away from the trapped side before a plan is valid.

  4. 04

    Decide risk first

    Risk belongs beyond the swept wick. Targets usually start at equilibrium or the opposite side of the candle range.

Range anatomy

One reference candle. Three levels.

Keep the markup quiet. The high, midpoint, and low are enough to see whether price is sweeping liquidity or simply expanding away without you.

Valid vs invalid

The filter should remove trades, not justify them.

If one part of the sequence is missing, the trade does not need a story. It needs to be skipped.

Valid setup

  • Clear CRH and CRL from a meaningful candle
  • Liquidity sweep beyond one side of the range
  • Re-entry back inside the range
  • Risk defined before entry

Invalid setup

  • Range chosen after the move already happened
  • Clean breakout being forced into a reversal idea
  • No displacement after re-entry
  • Stop or target moved to make the trade fit
Practice loop

Build the eye before you risk money.

Replay old sessions. Mark the range before the sweep. Save the chart. Score the result in R. After enough samples, the market tells you which sessions and ranges deserve attention.
