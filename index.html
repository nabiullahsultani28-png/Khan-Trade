What we teach

ICT is the framework.
CRT and Unicorn are how you trade it.

ICT (Inner Circle Trader) reads price the way institutions move it: every move is engineered to take liquidity and rebalance inefficiency. Learn that one idea and the chart stops looking random; it becomes a repeatable sequence.

1 Liquidity

Stops pile up above highs and below lows. That resting liquidity is the fuel price is drawn to.

2 The raid

Price spikes through a high or low to sweep that liquidity, trapping breakout traders. The trap is the setup.

3 Displacement

A sharp, one-sided move breaks structure and leaves an inefficiency (a Fair Value Gap) behind.

4 The return

Price retraces to the order block or FVG that caused the move. That retrace is your entry.

The building blocks

Six concepts do the heavy lifting.

Every ICT setup, including CRT and the Unicorn, is assembled from these pieces. Learn each one, and the rest is just sequencing them.

Market structure

Higher highs and lows read bullish; a Market Structure Shift is the first sign of a turn: the same accumulation → manipulation → distribution rhythm ICT calls the Power of Three.

Liquidity pools BSL / SSL

Stops cluster above equal highs and below equal lows. Price is engineered to reach that resting liquidity before it reverses.

Order blocks

The last opposing candle before a strong move marks where institutions loaded up, and price respects it on the return. A level that breaks and flips becomes a Breaker Block.

Fair Value Gaps FVG

A three-candle imbalance left by fast, one-sided movement. Price tends to return and rebalance it, so FVGs act as both entries and targets.

Premium & discount

Split any range at its midpoint. Sell from the premium half, buy from the discount half, entering at fair value instead of chasing.

Kill zones timing

ICT fires on schedule, during the London and New York sessions (the "Silver Bullet" windows). The same pattern outside them is mostly noise.
