Unicorn Model

Breaker and FVG overlap after the raid.

The ICT Unicorn Model is a confluence entry. Price first raids liquidity, then shifts structure with displacement. The trade idea is the retest where the Breaker Block and the Fair Value Gap overlap.

What it is

A retest model, not a breakout chase.

The Unicorn is usually taught as the point where a Breaker Block and Fair Value Gap overlap after price has taken liquidity and shifted away with displacement.

Important correction

The sweep gives context, but the entry is not the sweep itself. The entry is planned after displacement, when price returns into the overlap zone. If the overlap is not clear, the model is not clean enough to call a Unicorn.

01

Liquidity gets taken

Price raids a visible pool of buy-side or sell-side liquidity. This creates the trap that sets up the reversal or continuation idea.

02

Structure shifts with displacement

The move away from the raid should be decisive enough to break short-term structure and leave an imbalance.

03

Breaker and FVG overlap

The failed order block becomes the Breaker. The imbalance becomes the FVG. The shared area is the zone being tested.

The sequence

Six checks before the trade idea exists.

Each step filters weak setups out. The model starts with liquidity and ends only if the retest happens inside the Breaker and FVG overlap.

  1. 01

    Mark the draw on liquidity

    Start with obvious equal highs, equal lows, previous session extremes, or another clean liquidity pool.

  2. 02

    Wait for the raid

    Price should take that pool and reject it. Do not treat the first spike as the entry.

  3. 03

    Require MSS or structure break

    After the raid, price must shift away and prove the trapped side is losing control.

  4. 04

    Find displacement and the FVG

    The shift should leave a clear three-candle imbalance that can later be revisited.

  5. 05

    Map the Breaker Block

    The failed order block that price breaks through becomes the Breaker zone on the retest.

  6. 06

    Use only the overlap

    The actionable area is where the FVG and Breaker share price. Invalidation belongs beyond the structure that made the setup.

Valid vs invalid

The filter should reject most attempts.

The name sounds rare because the clean version needs several conditions to stack. That does not mean every imbalance after a sweep is a Unicorn.

Clean setup

  • Liquidity pool is marked before the move
  • Raid takes that pool and rejects
  • Structure shifts away with displacement
  • FVG is clear and overlaps the Breaker
  • Retest respects the overlap zone

Pass on it

  • The zone is identified only after the entry
  • No structure shift after the sweep
  • The FVG is tiny, messy, or far from the Breaker
  • Invalidation requires moving the stop
  • The target is not a logical liquidity draw
How it compares

CRT and Unicorn solve different problems.

CRT reads a reference candle range. The Unicorn reads a post-sweep retest into a Breaker and FVG overlap. Both are rule-based, but they do not trigger the same way.
