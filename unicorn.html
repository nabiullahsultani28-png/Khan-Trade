The Unicorn Entry Model

High confluence. Low risk.

The Unicorn is the highest-confluence ICT entry there is - a triple-confirmation setup that stacks a Breaker Block, a Fair Value Gap and a liquidity sweep into a single, precise entry with a tight stop. Rare by design - but when it forms, the reward-to-risk is exceptional.

What it is The three confluences CRT vs Unicorn
The idea in one minute

What is the Unicorn?

Most ICT entries lean on one or two signals. The Unicorn demands three at once - and only triggers where all three overlap at the same price. That overlap is what makes it so precise, and so rare.

Three things must align same price · same window
  1. 1

    Liquidity sweep the trigger

    Price raids equal highs or equal lows (BSL/SSL), grabbing the resting stops, then snaps back. Without a sweep first, there is no Unicorn.

  2. 2

    Breaker Block the origin

    A failed order block - the candle that built a swing high or low which price then swept and broke back through. When structure flips, that zone reverses polarity and becomes support (or resistance) on the return.

  3. 3

    Fair Value Gap the entry

    A 3-candle imbalance left by the displacement that sits inside or overlapping the Breaker. That overlap is the precision entry zone.

When the sweep, the Breaker and the FVG line up at one price, you enter inside the gap with your stop just beyond the swept extreme - defined risk, outsized reward.

Why "Unicorn"

Rare by design

It's called the Unicorn because a clean setup is uncommon - you might get 1–3 a week, almost always inside the kill zones. You wait for it; you never force it.

The payoff

Exceptional reward-to-risk

Because the stop sits just beyond the swept extreme and the target is the opposite liquidity, a clean Unicorn often pays 1:5 or better.

The three confluences

All three, or it isn't a Unicorn.

Each piece is a standard ICT concept on its own. The edge is insisting on all three in the same place - and passing on every setup that only has one or two.

Liquidity Sweep

A liquidity sweep is the raid on equal highs or lows (BSL/SSL) where stop orders rest - price spikes through, grabs them, then snaps back, trapping breakout traders. Without it first, there's no Unicorn.

Breaker Block

A Breaker Block is an order block that failed and flipped: price sweeps a swing high or low, then displaces back through it and breaks structure. The candle that marked that level now acts as support/resistance on the retest - a precise zone with a tight stop.

Fair Value Gap

A Fair Value Gap (FVG) is a three-candle imbalance left by fast, one-sided movement - a gap the market tends to return and rebalance. In a Unicorn it must sit inside or overlap the Breaker, pinning the exact entry.

Anatomy of a Unicorn

Sweep, breaker, gap - then go.

Price sweeps the sell-side liquidity below the lows, displaces up and breaks structure, leaving a Breaker Block that overlaps a Fair Value Gap. It retraces into that overlap - your entry - then expands to the buy-side liquidity above. This is a long example; flip it for shorts.

From pool to entry

The Unicorn, in four moves.

Like every ICT setup, it's a sequence - and if a step is missing, there's no trade. Here's the exact order, every time.

01

Identify a clean liquidity pool

Mark an obvious pool of equal highs or equal lows - the resting stops everyone can see. That's the bait.

02

Wait for the sweep

Price spikes through the pool, grabs the stops, and closes back inside. The raid is the trigger - not a breakout you chase.

03

Find the Breaker + FVG overlap

On the displacement away from the sweep, mark the Breaker Block and the 3-candle Fair Value Gap. The Unicorn is exactly where they overlap.

04

Enter inside the gap

Enter in the FVG within the Breaker. Stop just beyond the swept extreme; target the opposite liquidity - 1:3 RR minimum, often far more.

Timing

When the Unicorn appears

Unicorn setups form almost exclusively inside the London (02:00–05:00 NY) and New York AM (08:30–11:00 NY) kill zones, when institutional liquidity enters. Outside those windows there's nothing to do but mark levels and wait.

The edge

Why it works

Three independent ICT signals agreeing at one price is rare - and rare agreement is high-probability. You trade only the cleanest version of the sweep-and-return, and skip everything else.

How it compares

CRT vs the Unicorn.

Two rules-based ICT models, two different jobs. CRT gives you frequent, repeatable session trades; the Unicorn waits for the rare, high-confluence setup. Most traders learn both and let the market decide which is on offer.
